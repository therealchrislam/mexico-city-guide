La comida — the main meal

The big meal is a late, long lunch served 1:30-5pm (usually after 2:30). Many restaurants close at 6 or 7pm. On Sunday nights it's nearly impossible to find anywhere cool open. People in restaurants at 6pm are still there from lunch — that's "sobremesa." Ask for the check; bringing it without being asked is rude.

Altitude & water

Mexico City is 7,300 feet above sea level — expect possible nausea, dehydration, headache, shortness of breath. You're more likely to get sick from tap water than food. Don't brush teeth with tap water or swallow in the shower. Raw fruits that need peeling (mangos, bananas, watermelon) are always fine.

Taxis & Uber

Uber works great and is cheap — a total game changer. Street cabs: make sure it's a white sedan with red stripe (or burgundy/gold); license plate = capital letter + 5 numbers; meter must be on. Give the colonia AND nearest cross street, not just an address.

Credit cards & tipping

When paying by card, restaurants usually add the tip before running it. When you hand over your card, say "con quince" (15%). They'll mention it if you don't. "This causes a lot of confusion."

Ordering drinks

A "michelada" in DF is beer with lime on ice with salted rim — order by beer name: "Bohemia michelada." A "limonada" is fresh limeade, say "mineral" or "natural" for bubbles. Margaritas are mostly for tourists. Mezcal is hip and drunk straight. Martinis increasingly popular at nicer spots.

Basic greetings go a long way

Buenos días (before noon), buenas tardes (noon-dark), buenas noches. Say hi entering and bye leaving — you're ahead of 90% of tourists. Very polite culture. You're "norteamericano," not "americano." The city is "el DF."
